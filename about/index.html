I have a PhD in Health Economics obtained at City, University of London. My research work focused on the economic analysos of antibiotics. Essentially I was looking into estimating the impact of a wise use of antibiotics by individual GPs and what could policy makers do about it. In other terms: lots of econometrics, spatial and counter-factual analysis

.

I'm highly committed to learning, which I try to do every day (both at work and at home). I am currently working on deepening my SQL and version control skills.

I find the term Data Scientist exteremely confusing. I believe that every data analysis should start with asking why are we doing it and for who. Targeting an analysis to your audience is key. I am a big fan of reproducibility in both science and data analysis work. I get angry when I see complex algos been applied to data before understanding its key properties.

On a personal nopte I'm Italian, from Venice. I've lived in London for 5 roaring years, before moving back to Italy with my beautiful wife
